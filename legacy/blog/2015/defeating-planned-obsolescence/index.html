One of the weird things that have been happening over the past few weeks (okay fine - half a year) was that the garage door opener would constantly open halfway or so and then all the power would be cut (and it would stop). I've disregarded it since I could always continue opening it by tapping the machine (and the power would come back) or manually open the door. Today, the machine finally gave out and I decided to take a look at it.

At first, I thought that it was just the trip sensor that was loose, wire was loose, etc. Basically I was hoping that the machine itself wasn't broken. After all of that testing, I concluded that the machine inside was busted. What a bummer. Nobody wants a $300 dead weight.

The electrical engineer inside of me (okay fine I am in computer science but its close enough right?) decided to bust open the machine (after unplugging it of course!) to examine what's wrong. I took out my multimeter and started tracing the power and ground to see where it broke (since I remember that the machine completely lost power rather than parts of it not working). Turns out that the solder on one of the connectors went loose.

All in all, that easy fix costed me absolutely nothing (except for a dab of new solder) and if people knew just to take a look into their broken machines, they can save a lot of money.
